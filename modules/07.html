Module 07

Measurement & Monitoring

What to measure, how to measure it, and how to connect measurement to action. Leading indicators, PRAI as a measurement framework, composite scoring, and maturity progression.

~25 min
5 video clips

The most common measurement mistake I see? A CMO asks their team for "one number that shows how we're doing in AI." The team dutifully tracks total AI mentions across all engines and reports it monthly. That number is meaningless. It mixes awareness queries — where brands almost never appear — with evaluation queries where they do. It blends engines that behave completely differently into a single average. The data is dirty from day one, and every decision built on it is wrong.

The measurement discipline starts with scoping. I'm adamant about this: scope your prompts tightly to commercial intent within specific buying categories. If you ask ChatGPT "What is AI?" — it's not going to recommend a tool. That's an awareness-stage question. But "What are the best AEO agencies for B2B SaaS?" — that's an evaluation-stage query where brands appear on shortlists, where positioning matters, where the answer functionally replaces a sales conversation. That's where measurement matters.

Clip 01 What to Measure First — 5 min
Leading vs lagging indicators. The tracking cascade: index → impression → click → engagement → conversion. "Don't start with conversions."

Once you've scoped correctly, you need to understand the leading indicator cascade: indexed → impressions → clicks → engagement → conversion. Most teams skip straight to conversion, get a disappointing number three months later, and conclude "this doesn't work." That's like planting seeds on Monday and pulling them up on Friday to check for roots. The system teaches working the leading indicators first. If time-on-page is low, that tells you something specific — your content isn't engaging enough. If pages aren't getting indexed, you have a technical problem, not a content problem. Each stage is diagnostic. Each one tells you where to intervene.

Clip 02 PRAI as Measurement Framework — 6 min
Presence, Reputation, Alignment, Influence applied to metrics. "Websites control 82% of training data and 100% of retrieval."

You have to measure across engines. Our research across 600,000+ citations and over 1 million AI answers confirmed what we suspected: each engine operates completely differently. Citation patterns in ChatGPT don't predict citation patterns in Claude or Perplexity. They weight different ranking factors, respond to different freshness signals, and handle ambiguity in their own way. Your buyers use multiple engines, and the blind spots in any single one are invisible unless you're watching all of them.

Clip 03 Measurement Maturity Progression — 2.5 min
Keyword ranking → ad-hoc mention checking → systematic measurement → share-of-voice + sentiment. "95% of ChatGPT citations < 10 months old."

Think of it like the brand survey analogy. You can't measure a billboard's ROI directly. But you can survey for recall — "Have you seen this brand?" You can measure perception — "What do you associate with this brand?" You can check alignment — "Does this description match what the brand wants to be known for?" AI visibility works the same way. You measure whether AI "recalls" your brand in relevant contexts, positions you accurately, and describes you in ways that match your actual positioning. It's the same discipline applied to a new channel. And just like brand surveys, no single data point tells you much. The trend over time is what matters.

Clip 04 Composite Scoring — 3.5 min
Visibility + position + sentiment + consistency + cross-engine. Citation rate by vendor, four-quadrant market analysis.

There's one more piece that catches teams off guard: the freshness imperative. Content older than 30 days gets 3.2x fewer citations. That's not a rounding error — it's the difference between compounding and decaying. Ninety-five percent of ChatGPT citations reference content less than 10 months old. If your best content is a year-old whitepaper you haven't touched, it's fading out of AI answers right now.

The system teaches treating your site as a portfolio — borrowed from Jeff Wilkie's approach at Amazon. Every page is an asset. You manage which to invest in, which to refresh, and which to retire. The answer isn't fewer pages or all-manual updates — it's systematic portfolio management with AI agents handling freshness at scale. Update the data, refresh the examples, add the latest proof points. Keep the asset alive so it keeps compounding.

Clip 05 Connecting Measurement to Action — 3 min
Good-fit / bad-fit × high-intent / low-intent 2×2 framework. Deepgram 80% response rate from doc page visitors.

This is where most teams stall. They do an audit once, get a snapshot, and never look again. Measurement only works if it's ongoing — if it actually changes what you do next week. When you can pinpoint that indexing is the bottleneck (not content quality), or that Claude ranks you well but ChatGPT doesn't, you actually know where to intervene. That's also how you justify the budget: show the trend, show the cause, show the fix.

Key Takeaways

  • Start with leading indicators (indexed → impressions → clicks → engagement), not conversion. Conversion is a lagging indicator.
  • PRAI (Presence, Reputation, Alignment, Influence) applies to measurement just as it does to strategy.
  • Scope measurement to evaluation-stage queries within your buying categories — not vanity metrics across the funnel.
  • Measure across engines: ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity behave differently enough that single-engine data misleads you.
  • Treat your site as a portfolio — continuously monitor, refresh, and optimize. Content older than 30 days gets 3.2x fewer citations.
