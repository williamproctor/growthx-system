Module 02

AI Visibility Playbook

How AI engines decide what to cite, the three layers that control whether you show up, and the technical AEO work that gets your site into AI answers.

~30 min
3 video clips

Most people treat AI answers like magic. They type a question, get an answer, and never think about where it came from. But if you want to influence what AI says about your company, you need to understand the machinery underneath. It's not magic. It's infrastructure. And infrastructure can be shaped.

Three layers determine whether you show up in an AI answer. The first is pre-training — the massive data ingestion that happens before a model is released. Between 60% and 82% of that training data comes from crawled web pages. Your website, your blog posts, your documentation. That's the base layer of everything the model "knows." The second layer is real-time retrieval — when engines like Perplexity or ChatGPT with browsing pull fresh pages at query time. Here, 100% of what they surface comes from live web pages. The third layer is post-training preferences — RLHF, human feedback loops that teach the model which sources to trust and how to rank competing answers. Each layer plays by different rules, and each one gives you a different lever to pull.

Clip 01 How AI Engines Actually Work — 11 min
Three layers: pre-training (60-82% from web), retrieval, post-training preferences. Content flywheel. Freshness decay (30 days = 3.2× fewer citations).

Here's the part nobody talks about enough: your website is the atomic unit of AI visibility. Not your LinkedIn posts. Not your press releases. Not your social media presence. Your website — the thing you fully control, can update at any time, and can structure however you want. Social signals matter for distribution. But the raw material that AI engines consume? That's your site.

And here's the gap that keeps surprising us. When we audit companies at GrowthX — companies that rank beautifully in Google, that have strong SEO programs — over 50% have fundamental crawlability issues blocking AI engines. Cloudflare bot protection rules rejecting AI crawlers. CDN configurations serving blank pages to non-browser user agents. Robots.txt files that were written for Googlebot and accidentally block GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot. These companies are completely invisible to AI and have no idea. It's the most common technical gap we find, and it's usually a 30-minute fix once you know where to look.

Clip 02 The Four Ground Truths — 3.5 min
Jason's four truths: everything long-tail + personal, refresh more often, better distribution, AI converging with value not SEO.

Freshness is the other lever people underestimate. The data is stark: content older than 30 days gets 3.2x fewer AI citations. That's not a "nice to have when the team has bandwidth" metric. That's a cliff. AI engines, especially the retrieval layer, treat stale content as suspect. Pages that haven't been touched in months quietly drop out of citation pools. The fix is almost boring: swap in a recent stat, update an example, refresh the publish date. Do that every few weeks and your citation rate can triple.

Clip 03 Technical AEO + Authority Signals — 7.5 min
Schema metadata (73% of top-10 have it, 88% of sites don't), LLMs.txt, MCP servers, digital PR, E-E-A-T.

We frame all of this through four dimensions we call PRAI: Presence, Reputation, Alignment, Influence. Think of it like a brand survey, except the respondent is an AI engine. Does the model know you exist? (Presence.) Does it position you correctly against competitors? (Reputation.) Does it describe you the way you'd describe yourself? (Alignment.) Can you actually change what it says? (Influence.) Measure all four and you know exactly where you're strong, where you're invisible, and what to fix first.

Everything in the modules ahead, content strategy, quality systems, distribution, assumes you understand how these engines work. Skip this and you're optimizing blind. Nail it and the rest of the system clicks into place.

Key Takeaways

  • 60-82% of AI training data comes from crawled web pages. Your website is the atomic unit of AI visibility.
  • Content older than 30 days gets 3.2× fewer citations. Freshness is a lever, not a nice-to-have.
  • Over 50% of companies have technical issues blocking AI crawlers they don't even know about.
  • Schema markup and structured data aren't legacy SEO tactics. AI engines lean on them harder than Google ever did.
  • The visibility flywheel: quality content → gets crawled → gets cited → builds authority → earns more citations. It compounds, but only if you keep feeding it.
