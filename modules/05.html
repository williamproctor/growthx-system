Module 05

AEO Masterclass

Answer Engine Optimization from first principles. The 4-pillar maturity model, PRAI framework, and Webflow's credibility proof.

~40 min
6 video clips

Here's my biggest pet peeve in this space. Someone hears "AEO" for the first time and their immediate reaction is: "What tool do I buy?" That's backwards. That's like hearing "brand building" and asking which survey platform to subscribe to. The tool doesn't fix the problem. The discipline does.

AEO is a discipline — like content marketing, like brand strategy. You don't fix AI visibility by purchasing a better dashboard. You fix it by creating better content on the domain you control. The dashboard just tells you whether it's working. And if your content isn't genuinely useful, if nobody trusts you as a source — the dashboard is going to tell you the same bad news every month.

Here's the reframing: your first sales call now happens inside an AI answer. A buyer opens ChatGPT, spends 15–20 minutes getting personalized recommendations, comparing options, asking follow-ups about pricing and capabilities. That's a sales conversation. It's just happening without you in the room. If you're not in the answer, you've lost a deal you never even knew existed.

In the Webflow workshop, Guy Yalif laid out a maturity model with four dimensions. Each one moves from an old-world metric to a new one:

→ Content maturity: keywords → answers. Stop optimizing for keyword strings. Start creating content that directly answers the questions buyers are asking.
→ Technical maturity: on-page SEO → structured schema. AI engines need machine-readable signals — not just good copy.
→ Authority maturity: backlinks → mentions. Links still matter, but AI citation runs on mentions and trust signals that go well beyond PageRank.
→ Measurement maturity: ranking → share-of-voice. Position 1 on Google is one metric. Share-of-voice across AI engines tells you something rankings never could.

Most companies are stuck at level one across the board. They don't realize it because they're still measuring rankings and traffic. The maturity model shows you where you actually stand.

So how do you build the discipline? We use the PRAI framework — four dimensions that map your AI visibility:

→ Presence — Are you even showing up? Not once, not sometimes — consistently, across engines. ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Gemini. If you're visible in one and invisible in three, you have a presence problem.
→ Reputation — When you do show up, how are you described? Is the language accurate? Is it favorable? Or is AI surfacing three-year-old complaints from a review site you forgot about?
→ Alignment — Does what AI says about you match what you actually want to be known for? This is your biggest lever. The gap between your positioning and AI's perception of you is where you spend your energy.
→ Influence — Can you systematically improve all three over time? This isn't a one-time audit. It's an ongoing practice, and the companies that get results are the ones that keep at it quarterly, not annually.

The sphere of influence model makes this actionable. Start at the center — your website. You control it completely and it's where revenue happens. Then work outward to platforms you influence: LinkedIn, YouTube, GitHub, review sites. You can publish and engage there, but you don't own the algorithm. The outer ring is what you earn — analyst reports, press coverage, word of mouth. You start at the center because the outer rings need something real to reference.

Key Takeaways

  • AEO is a discipline, not a tool. Dashboards report the score. Content quality changes the score.
  • The 4-pillar maturity model (Content, Technical, Authority, Measurement) tells you exactly which dimension to fix first.
  • PRAI (Presence, Reputation, Alignment, Influence) is the operational framework for AI visibility.
  • Webflow went from 0% to 42% LLM traffic in one year with 6x conversion — proof the model works.
  • Your sphere of influence: control (your site) → influence (LinkedIn, YouTube) → earn (analyst reports, press).
