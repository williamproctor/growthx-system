Module 06

Starter Prompts & Artifacts

The artifacts that make AI outputs sound like you, not like everyone else. Company context, audience personas, writing guidelines, plus the Shaping Loop for iterating any AI draft until it actually lands.

~15 min
2 video clips
This module is mostly written, not video. Two short clips, then detailed guides on building each artifact and running the Shaping Loop.

I watch this happen constantly. A team member spends two weeks perfecting their article-writing prompt. Tweaking the system instructions. Adding constraints. Rearranging the order of operations. The output improves. Marginally. Meanwhile, another team member spends that same two weeks building a company context artifact, an audience persona doc, and a writing guideline. They use a dead-simple prompt, "write me an article about X," and the output is unrecognizable compared to the first person's. Same model, same topic. Wildly different result.

The difference isn't the prompt. It's the context.

I demo this live in workshops. Same generation, twice. Once with a bare prompt, once with context artifacts loaded. The first version reads like it could be about any company in any industry. The second version sounds like us. It references our positioning, uses language our buyers recognize, makes arguments we'd actually make. The prompt didn't change. The context did.

Clip 01 The Shaping Loop — 3.5 min
FLIP / SPREAD / ARTICULATE / CHISEL / RE-ANCHOR with before/after examples. "Context rot" stat (17-35% degradation after 10 turns).

Three artifacts do most of the heavy lifting:

→ Company context — Not your "About Us" page. I mean the real stuff. Origin story, current business reality, market position, founder philosophy, customer reviews verbatim, competitor perception. A shallow self-description gets you shallow content. If all the AI knows about you is "we're a B2B SaaS company that helps teams collaborate" — guess what kind of generic slop you're getting back.

→ Audience personas — Start with daily reality, not demographics. What does their actual job description look like? What work products do they deliver every week? What makes their day miserable? What triggers them to start evaluating solutions? Demographics tell you who they are on paper. Daily reality tells you what they care about at 2pm on a Tuesday.

→ Writing guidelines — The adjective spectrum exercise is worth the time. Where does your brand sit between "formal" and "casual"? Between "technical" and "accessible"? Between "provocative" and "measured"? Pair that with good/bad example pairs — "this sounds like us, this doesn't" — and you've given the model a voice it can actually hit. I compare these to cursor rules or claude.md files. They improve with every use. Every time you correct an output, you're refining the guideline.

Clip 02 Context Artifacts as RAG Inputs — 2 min
Positioning docs, customer quotes, competitor analysis. "Output isn't generic anymore — it references your actual differentiators."

Artifacts give you the raw material. Iteration is what turns it into something good. That's the Shaping Loop:

→ Flip the angle — reframe the perspective entirely. If the draft reads like a vendor pitch, flip it to the buyer's inner monologue.
→ Spread — explore alternative structures. Listicle, narrative, framework breakdown, case study. Same content, different architecture.
→ Articulate — sharpen the specific value. Vague claims become concrete mechanisms. "We help you grow" becomes "we reduced CAC by 40% in 90 days."
→ Chisel — cut everything that doesn't serve the reader. If a paragraph exists because it sounds smart but doesn't move the piece forward, kill it.
→ Re-Anchor — ground the output back in your artifacts and quality bar. Without this, everything slowly drifts toward generic.

Why does re-anchoring matter? After about 10 conversation turns, output quality drops 17–35%. We call this "context rot." The model drifts from who you are and what you're trying to say. Re-anchoring pulls it back to your source artifacts. A hard reset before the model wanders too far.

Key Takeaways

  • Context beats prompt engineering. The artifacts you feed the model matter more than how you phrase the ask. Watch the live demo in Module 04 →
  • Three artifacts do the work: company context, audience personas, writing guidelines.
  • The Shaping Loop (Flip / Spread / Articulate / Chisel / Re-Anchor) is how you iterate a draft from generic to yours.
  • Artifacts improve with use, like cursor rules or claude.md files. Every correction sharpens them.
  • Personas start with daily reality, not demographics. What does their Tuesday afternoon look like? Module 03 has the full audience-first framework →
